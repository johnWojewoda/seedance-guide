02

Camera & Motion Control

Most people typing prompts into Seedance 2.0 use the word "cinematic" and hope for the best. That word means nothing to the model. What does work is real cinematography language — the same language a director uses on a film set.

Seedance 2.0 has been trained on real films. When you speak to it like a director, it responds like one. This section teaches you exactly how to do that.

1

Lens Types

The lens you specify changes everything — depth of field, compression, how the background relates to the subject. Two lenses every Seedance 2.0 creator should know first:

35mm Anamorphic
Wide, dramatic, movie-like. Creates horizontal lens flares and a distinctive widescreen feel. Best for establishing shots and action.
"35mm anamorphic lens, wide establishing shot"
85mm Prime
Tight, intimate, portrait-style. Compresses the background and isolates your subject beautifully. Best for close-ups and emotional moments.
"85mm prime lens, shallow depth of field"
Macro
Extreme close-up detail. Perfect for product reveals, textures, and detail shots that would be impossible with a standard lens.
"macro lens, extreme close-up detail"
24mm Wide
Immersive and expansive. Creates a sense of environment and scale. Great for landscapes, architecture, and POV shots.
"24mm wide angle lens, expansive environment"
Beginner tip

Start with just 35mm anamorphic or 85mm prime until you are comfortable. Once you know how each one feels, experiment with the others.

2

Camera Movements

Camera movement creates emotion. A slow push-in builds tension. A pull-out creates isolation. Seedance 2.0 understands these movements when you name them correctly.

Slow push-in Camera moves slowly toward the subject. Builds tension and intimacy.
Pull-out Camera moves away from subject. Creates isolation or reveals scale.
Tracking shot Camera follows the subject from the side. Creates dynamic movement.
Orbit / 360 Camera circles the subject. Great for product reveals and character introductions.
POV First-person perspective. Immersive and visceral. Great for action sequences.
Handheld Subtle shake and imperfection. Feels documentary and real. Great for emotional scenes.
Beginner tip

Always combine a lens type with a movement. "85mm prime lens, slow push-in" gives Seedance 2.0 much more to work with than either one alone.

3

Shot Types

Shot types tell Seedance 2.0 how much of the scene to show. Used correctly they control pacing and storytelling within a single generation.

Wide shot Shows the full environment. Sets the scene.
Medium shot Waist up. The conversational standard.
Close-up Face or object fills the frame. High emotion.
Extreme close-up One detail fills the frame. Eyes, hands, objects.
Overhead Bird's eye view looking straight down.
Low angle Looking up at subject. Creates power and dominance.
Beginner tip

Use shot types inside brackets to direct Seedance 2.0 like a film director: [Shot 1: Wide] then [Cut to: Close-up]. This is how you build a scene within one generation.

4

Bracketed Shot Commands

This is where camera control becomes truly powerful. Seedance 2.0 can generate multiple shots in a single 15-second generation — but only if you use the correct bracketed syntax to direct it.

Single shot

[Shot 1: Wide] subject standing on clifftop, 35mm anamorphic, golden hour light

Two shot sequence

[Shot 1: Wide] subject walking through forest, tracking shot. [Cut to: Close-up] face emerging from shadow, 85mm prime, soft rim light

Three shot sequence

[Shot 1: Overhead] city at night, slow pull-back. [Cut to: Medium] figure on rooftop, 35mm anamorphic. [Cut to: Extreme close-up] eyes reflecting city lights below

Beginner tip

For your first attempts stick to one or two shots maximum. Seedance 2.0 handles motion physics best in clips of 6-8 seconds. After 12 seconds it can start to lose consistency.

Complete Camera & Motion prompt

[Shot 1: Wide] lone figure standing at edge of misty forest, 35mm anamorphic lens slow push-in, volumetric fog catching morning light. [Cut to: Close-up] 85mm prime, face turned upward, soft diffused light from above
