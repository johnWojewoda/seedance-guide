1

The Final QA Checklist

Every sequence must pass this audit before final export. Run through each item systematically — not as a quick scan, but as a deliberate frame-by-frame review. One missed detail at this stage can destroy the illusion in the final cut.

✓
Anchor Audit
Are all immutable anchors present in every shot? Check nose rings, hair color, dominant hand, scars, tattoos, and specific accessories frame by frame.
✓
Feature Scan
Scrub through each clip looking for pose flips and disappearing accessories. Pay particular attention to the final 2-3 seconds where drift is most likely.
✓
Seed Consistency
If you used seed locking across multiple clips, confirm the seed remained consistent during any re-renders. A single unlocked re-render can break character consistency across the entire sequence.
✓
Cross-Shot Lighting
Check for sudden color temperature jumps or specular shifts between cuts. These are invisible during generation but jarring in the assembled sequence.
✓
Temporal Coherence
Confirm the physics did not turn to soup in the final 3 seconds. Watch the last quarter of each clip carefully — this is where memory buffer degradation shows up first.
✓
Audio Review
Listen to each clip's audio in isolation. Does it match what you asked for? Does it fight the visuals or support them? Is there unwanted music or birdsong?
✓
Vmake Enhancement
Has every approved clip been run through Vmake's 4K+ enhancer? Never deliver a raw 480p or 720p Seedance output without enhancement.
Beginner tip

Do this checklist on a large screen, not a phone. Many drift artifacts are subtle at small sizes but immediately visible on a monitor. Always do your final QA at full resolution on the largest screen available.

2

When to Re-Generate vs When to Fix in Post

Not every problem requires a re-generation. Understanding which issues to fix in post-production versus which ones require going back to Seedance 2.0 is a crucial budget decision.

Fix in post Soft edges, digital grain, waxy skin texture, minor color temperature inconsistencies, slight blurriness. These are Vmake jobs — not re-generation jobs.
Re-generate Identity drift, pose flips, physics soup, mid-stride wardrobe changes, hallucinated characters, wrong subject in frame. These are prompt problems that no post tool can fix.
Refine the prompt first Before re-generating, diagnose the exact cause of the problem. A re-generation with the same broken prompt produces the same broken result. Fix the prompt, then re-generate.
Beginner tip

If you are re-generating the same clip more than twice, stop. The problem is in the prompt architecture — not the model. Go back to the Five-Part Spine, audit each element, and identify exactly what is missing or broken before spending another credit.

3

Color Grading AI Video

AI-generated video responds differently to color grading than traditionally shot footage. The synthetic nature of the pixels means that aggressive grading can expose artifacts that were invisible in the raw output. A subtle grade almost always looks better than a heavy one.

Grade after Vmake Always run Vmake enhancement before color grading. The enhancer fixes pixel-level artifacts that grading will otherwise amplify.
Avoid heavy contrast Heavy contrast grades expose the synthetic noise in shadow areas. Keep contrast adjustments subtle and use Vmake's grain removal to clean shadows before grading.
Match across clips Use the same LUT or grade on every clip in a sequence. Even slight variations in grade between clips will break the consistency you worked so hard to achieve in generation.
Beginner tip

The best AI video color grades are barely perceptible. You are not transforming the image — you are refining it. Aim for a grade that makes the viewer feel the scene, not one that announces itself visually.
